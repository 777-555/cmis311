CHAPTER 7 DESCRIPTION This chapter discusses important business skills for technical professionals. Skills like listening, communicating, and writing are necessities. Other skills like customer service, time management, and project management are also valuable. As technical professionals grow in their role, skills in financial and human resources management become more important. Business skills yield a more capable inudvidual.
KEY WORDS Key Terms abandon rate—The percentage of abandoned calls compared to the total number of calls Received. abandoned call—A call where the caller hangs up before an analyst answers. acceptance criteria—The conditions that must be met before the project deliverables are accepted. business and IT alignment—A process aimed at ensuring that information technologies support corporate goals and objectives. change control plan—A description of how the project scope will be managed and how agreed upon changes will be incorporated into the project deliverables. Gantt chart—A type of bar chart that is often used to illustrate a project schedule intangible—A characteristic that is difficult to measure precisely, such as customer satisfaction, employee satisfaction, and quality. scope creep—A term used to describe unplanned changes to a project’s scope. work breakdown structure (WBS)—A task-oriented breakdown of the work to be done.
