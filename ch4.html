Summary Technology helps the customer service department communicate with customers. The Internet, email, and online chat make information sharing and organization more convenient. Enterprises use these tools to provide self-service to customers. Good writing skills help efficiently acquire and share information. Before writing, it is very important to understand the target audience and purpose. Following documentation standards ensures accuracy and consistency. Writing improves through practice. Learning writing rules makes things easier. Best practices in technical writing and email help professionals communicate clearly.
Key Words capture—To collect. case-based reasoning (CBR)—A searching technique that uses everyday language to ask users questions and interpret their answers extranet—A web site that is accessed via the Internet—that is, it can be accessed by the general public—but requires a password to gain entry to all or parts of the site. fuzzy logic—A searching technique that presents all possible solutions that are similar to the search criteria, even when conflicting information exists or no exact match is present. hypermedia—A storage method that stores information in a graphical form so users can access the information in a nonlinear fashion using hyperlinks. integrated ITSM solution—A suite of systems that companies use to manage their incident, problem, knowledge, change, service asset and configuration management, and request fulfillment processes; sometimes called enterprise solutions. query by example (QBE)—A searching technique that uses queries, or questions, to find records that match the specified search criteria; can include search operators.
