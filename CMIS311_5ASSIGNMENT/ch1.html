A brief description of the main content and theme of the chapter:
Chapter 1: Achieving High Customer Satisfaction focuses on the role of the service desk in delivering high-quality technical support.
This chapter discusses the importance of the service desk. In an increasingly technology-dependent world, the service desk serves as a single point of contact to manage customer issues and service requests.
This chapter outlines the four key elements of a successful service desk, evolving technical support trends, and customer expectations.
It also highlights the skills required for a technical support career, including business, technical, software, and self-management skills.
It also explores the transition from help desk to service desk, the impact of Information Technology Infrastructure Library (ITIL), and the strategic role of the service desk in running an enterprise.
A glossary of terms that were new to me in the chapter:
- automatic call distributor (ACD)—"A telephone technology that answers a call and routes, or distributes, it to the next available analyst. If all analysts are busy, the ACD places the call in a queue and plays a recorded message."
- configuration management system (CMS)—"A set of tools and databases for managing IT asset information and linking that information to related incidents, problems, known errors, changes, and releases."
- customer service value chain—"A linked set of activities during which value is added when serving customers."
- Information Technology Infrastructure Library (ITIL)—"A set of best practices for IT service management."
- ISO/IEC 20000—"An international standard for IT service management."
- knowledge base administrator (KBA)—"Another name for a knowledge engineer."
- Knowledge-Centered Support (KCS)—"A knowledge management strategy for service and support organizations developed by the Consortium for Service Innovation."
- knowledge engineer—"A person who develops and oversees the knowledge management process and ensures that the information contained in the service desk’s knowledge management system is accurate, complete, and current; also called a knowledge base administrator (KBA)."
- knowledge management system (KMS)—"A set of tools and databases that are used to store, manage, and present information sources such as customer information, documents, policies and procedures, incident resolutions, and known errors."
- known error database (KEDB)—"A database that contains known error records."
- level zero—"Customers resolving incidents on their own; also referred to as tier zero or self-help."
- Operational Level Agreement (OLA)—"An agreement between internal support groups that underpins a Service Level Agreement by ensuring that all parties involved in meeting SLA targets understand their respective responsibilities."
- remote control and diagnostic systems—"Systems that allow the service desk to take remote control of the keyboard, screen, or mouse of connected devices and then troubleshoot problems, transfer files, and even provide informal training by viewing or operating the customer’s screen."
- subject matter expert (SME)—"A person who has in-depth knowledge about a particular process, function, or technology."
- swarming—"A collaborative approach in which a group of service desk analysts work together to resolve a complex issue instead of escalating it through the traditional tiered support model."
- Web 2.0—"A term used to describe the second generation of the internet, characterized by user-generated content, interactivity, and collaboration."
